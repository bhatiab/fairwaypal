Attending Your First F1 Race

A practical, honest guide for first-timers — from buying tickets to surviving race day.

Buying Tickets

Where to Buy

Always buy from the official circuit ticket partner or Formula One Group directly. Avoid third-party resellers — prices are inflated and fraud is common.

Which Grandstand?

For first-timers, the main grandstand or start/finish straight offers the most action. You'll see the grid, pit stops, and the race start. Check each race's grandstand guide on the race page.

3-Day vs 1-Day Tickets

A 3-day pass gives you Friday practice, qualifying on Saturday, and the race on Sunday. A Sunday-only ticket saves money but you'll miss qualifying — often as exciting as the race itself.

What to Expect

  • Cars are MUCH louder than you expect — bring foam earplugs or ear defenders
  • Races are around 90 minutes but a full race day is 6–8 hours
  • Bring food and water — circuit food is expensive
  • Phone signal is poor during peak times — download offline maps
  • The podium ceremony after the race is worth staying for

Race Weekend Structure

Friday — Practice

Two 60-minute practice sessions. Less busy, great for exploring the circuit and finding your grandstand.

Saturday — Qualifying

The most intense 60 minutes in motorsport. Q1, Q2 and Q3 knockout sessions determine the starting grid.

Sunday — Race Day

The main event. Arrive early — gates open 3–4 hours before the race and the atmosphere builds throughout the morning.

Packing Essentials

  • Ear protection (mandatory for your hearing)
  • Sunscreen SPF 50+
  • Rain poncho or lightweight jacket
  • Comfortable closed-toe shoes
  • Portable phone charger
  • Snacks and a refillable water bottle
