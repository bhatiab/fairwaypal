## Key gotchas and copy angles (for content writer)

These are the practical, non-obvious insights worth building copy around:

1. **Rain is not a risk — it is the plan.** Le Mans in May averages 16 rain days in the month. Multiple attendee reports describe it as "default wet." Pack as if it will rain, not in case it rains.

2. **This is the biggest MotoGP crowd on the calendar.** 311,797 in 2025, 297,471 in 2024. Sunday has 119,000+ people. Mobile networks fail. Agree meeting points before you enter.

3. **Hotels sell out 6–12 months in advance.** Not an exaggeration — it is the most-attended MotoGP race. If you don't have accommodation, camping at the circuit is the practical fallback, not a last resort.

4. **Free parking is included in 3-day tickets** — but race-day traffic is severe. The tram (T1) or shuttle from the city is strongly recommended over driving on Sunday.

5. **The tram is underused by visiting fans.** Line T1 from "Gares" stop (the train station area) runs every few minutes and reaches Antarès in ~20 minutes. Faster and far less stressful than driving.

6. **You cannot bring your own alcohol** — but you also cannot bring a sealed plastic bottle into the grandstands (must be open). Buy drinks inside or drink before entry.

7. **Arrive by 9am.** The circuit is 75% full by 8am on Sunday. Parking areas closest to the circuit fill fastest.

8. **Post-race exit is very slow.** Plan to stay at the circuit after the race — watch Moto2 podium, fan zone — rather than trying to leave immediately. Two hours of gridlock reported in forum posts.

9. **The Dunlop Chicane is the primary overtaking spot.** If you have GA, this is the section to position yourself for. T5 Goodyear grandstand covers T1–T3 and the bridge — recommended for the best all-round view of the action.

10. **Paris CDG has a direct TGV connection to Le Mans** — no need to go into central Paris. Journey ~1h 40min. Book via SNCF Connect or Trainline; advance fares from ~€15–€30.

## Notes for content improvement

- The circuit is 5–6 km south of Le Mans city, entirely within the broader Circuit de la Sarthe complex used for the 24 Hours. The Bugatti Circuit uses the infield roads — this context is useful for orientation (fans who've watched the 24h on TV will recognise the Dunlop bridge).
- Nantes is the other nearest major airport (~165 km) — worth mentioning for fans coming from UK on budget airlines.
- The "Automobile Club of the Sarthe" membership trick (joins on Saturday, gives covered grandstand access on Sunday) is worth mentioning in mistakes if it can be verified as still current practice.
- Le Mans city centre has a historic medieval old town (Cité Plantagenêt) — worth a sentence in the planner page for non-race days.
- GetYourGuide likely has guided tours of Le Mans / the 24h circuit — check offers.ts for IDs or research separately.
