# Assen MotoGP (Dutch TT) First-Time Attendee — Fact Check

Researched: April 2026
Source: Web research via AsssenMotoGP.com, SitWhere.com, GrandPrixGetaway, TT Festival site, Wikipedia

---

## Circuit history

- Oldest event on MotoGP calendar, running continuously since 1925
- First race July 11, 1925 on 28.4 km road circuit between Rolde, Borger and Schoonloo
- FIM World Championship status since inaugural 1949 season
- Purpose-built TT Circuit opened 1955
- Race moved from Saturday to Sunday in 2016 (tradition held from 1925–2015)
- 2025 marked 100 years of racing at Assen

## Circuit capacity

- Race day attendance: approximately 110,000
- Seated capacity: approximately 60,000
- Heavy Dutch and German crowd with large motorcycle-riding contingent

## Grandstands (verified from official circuit map)

- **Haarbocht**: 9,800 seats, 350m long, uncovered. Views from start/finish into Turns 1-5. PRIMARY OVERTAKING ZONE. **CRITICAL TIP: Book row 15 or higher — rows below 15 look through wire safety fence.**
- **Hoofd**: Only fully covered grandstand. Start/finish, pit lane, podium.
- **Geert Timmer**: Turns 15-16, final sector before main straight. Good value.
- **Winterdijk**: 9,100 seats, rebuilt 2018. High-speed Turns 15-18 section. Far left has partial pit views.
- **Ossebroeken**: Lower-cost, mid/upper unobstructed, lower rows have fence interference.
- **Stekkenwal, De Bult, TT World, Strubben** — other grandstand zones on circuit map
- Grass embankments throughout infield: open to ticket holders during practice/qualifying. Camping chairs permitted; tents/beach shelters NOT permitted.

## Transport (verified)

- **Amsterdam Centraal to Assen**: Change at Zwolle, total 2-2.5 hours, NS trains (NS app or OV-chipkaart)
- **Groningen to Assen**: Direct, 15-20 minutes. Groningen is best overflow base.
- **Assen NS station to circuit**: ~6 km.
  - Bus Line 1: direct to Jack Middelburgplein (north side of circuit), 7 minutes
  - Race weekend shuttle buses: Assen NS station → Noordlus entrance
- **Driving**: Via A28 motorway. Circuit parking free and marshal-directed. Opens 05:00 race day.
- **Motorcycles**: Dedicated parking areas. Very common to arrive by bike.
- **Camping**: On-site and nearby (Camping de Ommekeer). Global-Tickets Village camping ~€80/person for full weekend. Max 15 minutes walk to circuit.

## Rules (verified)

- Glass containers and alcohol brought from outside PROHIBITED (security checks bags)
- Limited outside food/drink: one non-alcoholic bottle, sandwich, snack
- No tents or beach shelters on grass banks (camping chairs OK)

## TT Festival

- Nine-day festival across Assen city centre running concurrently with race weekend
- Five live music stages, fair, drag racing, bars open late
- Market square (Markt) is focal point
- Northern Europe's largest free festival
- Many repeat attendees consider this the best part of the whole trip

## Weather (late June / early July)

- Average high: ~21°C (69°F) in late June
- Rainfall: ~84mm in June (~15 rain days), ~94mm in July (~16 rain days)
- Dutch weather: overcast and mild is default; warm sunny periods possible but unreliable
- Wet days make grass bank areas muddy
- Hoofd is the only covered grandstand

## Local food

- Bitterballen with Dutch mustard: standard Dutch bar snack
- Stroopwafel: best bought fresh/warm at market stall
- Haring (raw herring with onion and pickles): street stands, authentic Dutch
- Dutch asparagus (witte asperges): Drenthe province, may be end of season by late June
- Erwtensoep (split pea soup): more winter dish but available traditional restaurants
- Café options on Markt: Grandcafé Zusjes de Boer, Restaurant & Grandcafé Liff
- Pannenkoekenschip Assen: Dutch pancakes on a canal boat
